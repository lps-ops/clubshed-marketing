# ClubShed Distribution and Backlink Strategy

Where to share ClubShed, in what order, with what message. Built specifically for the grassroots football audience. No generic SEO advice, no buying backlinks.

## The honest reality first

Ranking takes time. Google indexes new sites quickly but trusts them slowly. Two things move the needle:

1. **Content that earns genuine links** (because it's useful to someone who would link to it)
2. **Distribution that puts your site in front of the right audience** (which generates real traffic, which generates links and brand searches, which compounds)

Most "SEO strategy" guides ignore the second one entirely and just tell you to "build backlinks." For ClubShed, distribution matters more than link-building in the first 6 months. You have a small budget (time, mostly), you're solo, and your audience is small and specific. Hit them directly where they are. Links follow.

The plan below is roughly chronological. Don't try to do everything at once.

---

## Phase 1: Set the foundation (week 1)

Before doing any outreach, the site needs to actually be findable.

### Submit to Google Search Console
- Add `clubshed.pro` as a property (verify via DNS TXT record in Cloudflare)
- Submit the sitemap at `clubshed.pro/sitemap.xml`
- Use the URL inspection tool to manually request indexing for the homepage, both blog posts, the comparison page, and the about page
- Set the preferred domain (clubshed.pro, not www)

### Submit to Bing Webmaster Tools
- Bing handles 5-10% of search traffic depending on country (higher in the US, lower in Germany)
- Free, takes 5 minutes, gives you indexing visibility on a search engine Google can't see

### Add Cloudflare Web Analytics
- Free, privacy-friendly, no GDPR cookie banner needed
- Tells you which pages get traffic and from where
- Critical because you can't optimise what you can't measure

### Set up email properly
- `hello@clubshed.pro` via Cloudflare Email Routing (you've already done this)
- Set up an email signature linking to clubshed.pro - every email you send becomes a soft promotion

## Phase 2: Direct distribution (weeks 1-4)

This is where most of your initial traction will come from. Real humans in the right communities. No SEO required.

### Reddit (highest leverage in week 1)

The grassroots football audience is on Reddit in numbers. Approach by being a member of the community, not by dropping links.

**Communities to join and post in (in this order):**

1. **r/grassrootsfootball** (~4k members) - your most direct audience. Post the end-of-season audit blog post with the framing "I wrote up what we do at my club, curious how others handle this." Don't sell.

2. **r/coachingyouthfootball** (~12k members) - coaches who deal with equipment. Same approach with a different post angle ("how do you stop losing your training balls?").

3. **r/footballcoaching** (~8k members) - similar audience.

4. **r/Volunteer** and **r/sportsmanagement** - tangential but relevant.

5. **r/SaaS** and **r/SideProject** - tech crowd, not your buyer, but get feedback and occasional backlinks from product writeups.

**The rules of posting on Reddit without getting banned:**
- Read the subreddit's rules before posting (some ban self-promotion entirely)
- Comment on other people's posts for a week before posting your own
- Frame your post as a question or experience share, not a product launch
- Mention ClubShed once in passing, not as the focus
- Reply to every comment personally
- Never use "I built ClubShed because..." as the opener

**Sample post structure:**
> Title: How do you stop losing your training balls?
>
> Body: I co-founded a grassroots club and we replace 6-8 balls a season because nobody knows where they went. I wrote up what actually worked for us (after a lot of failed approaches). Curious what you do at your club. [link to blog post]

This post format invites discussion, shares value, mentions the link once, doesn't read as marketing.

### Facebook Groups (lower leverage, higher reach)

A lot of grassroots clubs in the UK and Germany run on Facebook. Active groups to find:

- Search "grassroots football coaches UK" for FA-affiliated groups
- "FA Charter Standard Clubs" - many county-level versions
- "Grassroots Football [city]" for major UK cities
- "Verein und Trainer DFB" type groups for German clubs
- Country-specific: "Football Volunteers Ireland", "Football Coaching Australia"

Approach is the same as Reddit. Read, comment, contribute for a week, then share your blog post once with the same "wrote this up at my club" framing.

### LinkedIn (slow burn, high authority signal)

LinkedIn isn't where you'll find direct customers but it's where you'll be found by:
- Football association staff who might write about you later
- Sports SaaS journalists
- Other indie founders who might link to ClubShed in their newsletters

What to do:
- Update your profile to mention ClubShed prominently
- Post once a week. Something concrete you learned building or running the club. Not promotional, more like "I noticed this thing about how clubs handle X."
- Tag relevant people occasionally (not in a spammy way) when sharing something they'd care about
- The first three posts won't get traction. The 12th might.

### Direct email outreach (highest conversion, slowest scale)

This is where the real magic happens for early-stage products.

**Who to email:**
- 20 club secretaries you can find via county FA websites (each FA lists clubs with contact info publicly)
- 10 grassroots football bloggers and podcasters (search "grassroots football podcast" and "grassroots football blog")
- 5 sports SaaS comparison sites (more on these in Phase 4)

**What to say:**
- Short. Three paragraphs max.
- Specific to them, not a template (or at least clearly customised).
- Doesn't ask them to sign up.
- Asks for a 15-minute conversation, not a "review of my product."

Sample:
> Subject: 15 minutes with someone running a Hampshire grassroots club?
>
> Hi [name],
>
> I co-founded HVV Football Factory and built ClubShed (clubshed.pro), a tool for tracking the equipment that grassroots clubs lose every season. Before I add features, I'm spending two weeks talking to people running real clubs about what their actual process looks like.
>
> Hampshire FA links to your club on their site, which is how I found you. Would you have 15 minutes this week or next for a quick call about how your club handles kit and equipment? No pitch, genuinely just research.
>
> Worst case you spend 15 minutes telling someone about a problem you have anyway.
>
> Lucas

Response rate on emails like this is usually 20-30% for warm-niche audiences. From 20 emails you get 5 conversations. From 5 conversations you get 1-2 people who become users and tell other clubs about you. That's how grassroots traction starts.

## Phase 3: Earned media and content (weeks 2-8)

### Guest posts and contributed articles

The single highest-quality backlink source for ClubShed is football coaching and grassroots sports publications. They have authority, audience, and they actively need content.

Target list (in order of approach difficulty):

**Tier 1 (most realistic in month 1-2):**
- **Football Forums and Community Blogs** - sites like FootballCoaching.tv, CoachingFootballTactics.com, smaller WordPress blogs run by individuals. Email the editor with a specific pitch: "I wrote a guide on running an end-of-season equipment audit. Would your audience find it useful?" Offer to either repurpose your existing post or write a fresh one.

**Tier 2 (try in month 2-3):**
- **Coaches Voice** (coachesvoice.com) - tactical football publication. Not equipment-focused but accepts guest pieces.
- **Grassroots Football** magazines (search "grassroots football magazine UK") - some accept guest articles.
- **The Coaching Manual** - UK-based, content-hungry, might accept a piece on club operations.

**Tier 3 (long shot but worth trying):**
- **The Athletic** - grassroots series. Pitch a personal essay on the kit man's role at a grassroots club.
- **The FA's official blog** - they sometimes feature community stories.
- **DFB's grassroots content** for German audience.

### Help A Reporter Out (HARO) equivalents

Sign up for:
- **Connectively** (formerly HARO) - journalists looking for sources
- **Featured.com** - similar service
- **Qwoted** - PR pitches

Set filters for: grassroots sports, club management, sports volunteering, football, youth football. You'll get 3-10 relevant queries a week. Respond to journalists asking questions about grassroots football operations. Each response that gets quoted is a high-authority backlink and brand mention.

This is dramatically underused by small SaaS founders and produces some of the best links you can get.

### Podcast appearances

Search:
- "grassroots football podcast" on Apple Podcasts, Spotify
- "youth football coaching podcast"
- "club football podcast" (different from English club football which is the pro game)
- "sports volunteering podcast"

Email hosts: "I co-founded a grassroots club and built a tool that helps clubs track equipment. Would your audience find that interesting?"

3-5 podcast appearances in your first 6 months is a realistic goal. Each one is a backlink in show notes plus an audience of exactly your target buyer.

These are passive backlinks that compound. Submit once, get a slow trickle of traffic and authority over time.

- Product Hunt (when ready for a proper launch — see Phase 5)

- Indie Hackers Products section
- Crozdesk

- SaaSHub

**Sports-specific directories:**
- Sports Tech World (sportstechworld.com)
- Sports Innovation Lab

**UK and EU business directories:**
- These are spammy historically but Google still rewards a few canonical ones:
  - Hotfrog UK
  - Yell.com

## Phase 5: The Product Hunt launch (month 3-4)

Don't launch on Product Hunt until everything is solid:
- App actually works end-to-end with the dashboard, inventory, check-out flow
- Marketing site is fully built (which it now is)
- Payment is wired up
- You have 5-10 real users who can leave honest reviews on launch day

A successful Product Hunt launch generates:
- 1,000-5,000 visitors in the launch day
- Several high-authority backlinks
- Lasting Google indexing signal
- 10-30 actual signups

But a failed PH launch (under 50 upvotes) does nothing. Time it well.

**Launch checklist before pressing the button:**
- Tagline under 60 characters
- 5 high-quality screenshots, mobile and desktop
- A 30-second video demo (can be simple Loom screen recording)
- Hunters lined up (people who'll upvote in the first hour)
- Founder's response to every single comment, in the first 24 hours
- Launch on a Tuesday or Wednesday (highest engagement)
- Launch at 12:01am Pacific time

## Phase 6: Compounding content (months 2-12)

Beyond your 5 cornerstone posts, build content that earns links over time.

### High link-earning content formats

1. **Original research / data posts.** "We surveyed 50 grassroots football clubs about equipment loss. Here's what we found." Other blogs cite original data. You can run a simple Typeform survey in your audience and turn the results into a post that people link to.

2. **Comprehensive guides that update.** The grants guide you have planned should become the definitive guide to football equipment grants in the UK and Europe, updated each year. People link to comprehensive resources.

3. **Free tools.** A simple "equipment loss calculator" web tool ("How much is your club losing per season?") that anyone can use. Free tools attract links from people who include them in their roundups.

4. **Templates.** The Google Sheets inventory template you're already planning. Templates get linked everywhere.

5. **Counterintuitive takes.** A blog post arguing "you should NOT use Spond for equipment tracking" gets shared because it goes against conventional wisdom. Be careful — the take needs to actually be defensible.

### What NOT to write

- "10 ways to manage your football club better" - generic listicles get ignored
- "Top 5 inventory tools" comparison posts that include yours - looks self-serving
- AI-generated long-form fluff - Google penalises this now
- "Ultimate guide to football" - too broad, no specific intent
- Anything published just for SEO with no real content

## Backlink quality, not quantity

A single link from The Athletic, The FA's blog, or a popular grassroots football publication is worth 100 links from low-quality directory sites. Spend time chasing one good link, not building 50 bad ones.

Quality signals Google cares about:
- The linking domain's authority (Domain Rating, Domain Authority)
- The linking page's topical relevance (a football site linking to ClubShed is gold; a generic SaaS directory is meh)
- The link's placement (in body text > sidebar > footer)
- The link's anchor text (descriptive natural language > "click here")
- Whether the link is editorial (chosen by the author) or syndicated (auto-included on every page)

Don't:
- Buy links (Google detects and penalises)
- Use link exchange schemes
- Pay for guest post placement on sketchy sites (you'll be marked as spam)
- Comment-spam blogs with your URL
- Submit to 1000 directories

Do:
- Send 1 thoughtful pitch to a tier-1 publication every week
- Respond to 3-5 HARO queries per week
- Make sure every blog post is something someone might naturally want to link to

## Realistic 6-month outlook

**Month 1:** Maybe 5-20 visitors per day. Most from your direct outreach and Reddit. Zero Google traffic.

**Month 2:** 30-100 visitors per day. Google starts indexing properly. First organic traffic on long-tail queries.

**Month 3:** 100-300 visitors per day. First high-intent search traffic. Some user signups from organic.

**Month 4-5:** First guest post appearances if you've been pitching. Backlink profile starts looking real.

**Month 6:** 500-2000 visitors per day if cornerstone content is ranking and you've earned a few good backlinks. First "real" SEO traffic.

If you hit different numbers — much higher or much lower — adjust. But don't expect SEO to kick in fast. Trust the process.

## Things to track

Use a simple spreadsheet, not a SEO tool subscription. You don't need the depth yet.

- **Weekly:** Total visitors (from Cloudflare Web Analytics), signups (from Lovable), revenue (when payments are live)
- **Monthly:** Top 10 organic keywords, top 10 referring sites, total backlinks (use Ahrefs free Webmaster Tools)
- **Quarterly:** What worked, what didn't, what to double down on
